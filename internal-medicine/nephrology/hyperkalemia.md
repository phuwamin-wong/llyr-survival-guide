# Hyperkalemia

## Definition

* Serum potassium ≥ 5.5 mmol/L
* Severity grading
  * Mild = 5.5 - 6.0 mmol/L
  * Moderate = 6.1 - 7.0 mmol/L
  * Severe > 7.0 mmol/L

## Patient evaluation

* Rule-out emergency condition
  * Arrhythmia
    * EKG changes หากมีถือว่าเป็น Emergency\*\*\*
      * Tall peaked T
      * Flattened T wave, prolonged PR interval, depressed ST segment
      * Atrial standstill
      * Prolonged QRS duration
      * Sinusoid pattern

## Approach

* **Rule-out** pseudohyperkalemia: Prolonged tourniquet time, hemolysis, WBC > 2

{% hint style="info" %}
ถ้า Hemolysis ≥ 2+ -- > repeat lab Electrolyte
{% endhint %}

* **Find** causes of **Extracellular shift of K**
  * **Propranolol, Digitalis, Succinylcholine**
* **True hyperkalemia**
  * GFR ≤ 20 --> AKI, CKD
  * GFR ≥ 20
    * **High K intake**
    * **Hyperkalemic RTA (type IV RTA)**
    * Primary hypoaldosteronism (Addison's disease)
    * Secondary hypoaldosteronism (hyporeninemic hypoaldosteronism)
      * DM
      * Obstructive uropathy
      * Tubulointerstitial disease
      * SLE
      * HIV
    * Drugs:
      * NSAIDs, ACEi/ARB, Heparin
      * Spironolactone, Amiloride, Triamterene, TMP/SMX

## Management

* Should EKG 12 leads (esp ≥ 6 mmol/L)
* **EKG change?**
  * 10% Calcium gluconate 10 mL IV drip in 10 min
* Lower K in serum
  * **Intracellular shift of K**
    * RI 10 unit + Glucose 50 mL IV slowly push (if POCT-glucose < 250 mg%)
    * Salbutamol 4 NB (10 mg) NB stat
  * **GI removal of K**
    * Kalimate 30 g + น้ำ 50 mL PO q 4 hr x 1 - 3 doses (ขึ้นกับ degree of hyperkalemia)
    * หากกินไม่ได้: Kalimate 30 g + น้ำ 300 mL RS q 4 hr x 1 - 3 doses (ขึ้นกับ degree of hyperkalemia)
  * **Renal removal of K**
    * Furosemide starting dose ขึ้นกับ creatinine level
  * **Hemolysis**
