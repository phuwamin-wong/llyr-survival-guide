## Antibiotics in Several Clinical Diagnosis

### Community-acquired Pneumonia

* **OPD**\
  Amoxicillin or doxycycline or macrolides (azithromycin /clarithromycin)
  * If having comorbidities (chronic heart/lung/liver kidney, DM,\
    alcoholism, malignancy, asplenia)
    * combination therapy: Augmentin/Cephalosporin + Macrolide/Doxycycline
    * Monotherapy: respiratory fluoroquinolone (Levofloxacin/Moxifloxacin)
* **IPD**
  * B-lactam + Macrolides:
    * Cefriaxone 2 gm IV OD + Azithromycin IV
  * Monotherapy: respiratory FQ IV
* **ICU**\
  Combination only: B-lactam + Macrolides/respiratory FQ
* **Aspiration pneumonia:**\
  Augmentin 1.2 gm IV q 8 hr

### Nosocomial Infection

* **Anaerobic bacteria**: Tigecycline
* _Acinetobacter baumannii:_
  * Carbarpenem or sulbactam\
    (if resist to Carbapenem use Colistin then Tigecycline)
* _Pseudomonas aeruginosa_: Ceftazidime 2 gm IV q 8 hr + Gentamicin 7\
  MKD OD, Levofloxacin, Tazocin, Carbapenem, Fosfomycin
* **ESBL**: Carbapenem or Pipperacillin/tazobactam or Colistin or Tigecycline
* **MRSA**: Vancomycin, Linezolid, Fosfomycin, Tigecycline
* **Vancomycin-resistant enterococci (VRE)**: Linezolid, Tigecycline

### Diarrhea

* _Vibrio cholerae_: Tetracycline 500 mg qid x3 days / Ciprofloxacin 1 g single dose
* Shigella spp.: Ciprofloxacin 500 mg PO bid 5 days / Norfloxacin 400 mg PO bid
* Salmonellosis (dysentery, fever): Ciprofloxacin 500 mg PO bid 10-14 day / 3rd generation\
  Cephalosporin.
  * For carrier: Norfloxacin 400 mg PO bid 28 days\
    ⚠️ ถ้ามีแค่ enteritis อาจไม่ต้องให้
* E. coli: พิจารณาให้เป็นราย ๆ (ETEC: Ciprofloxacin/Norfloxacin 3-5 days)
* Campylobacter spp.: Erythromycin 250 – 500 mg PO qid 7 days
* _Clostridium difficile_: Metronidazole 400 mg PO tid 7-10 day
  * If severe (WBC ≥15,000 or Cr >1.5)
    * Vancomycin (125) 1 tab po pc qid x 10\
      days (oral)
* _Entamoeba histolytica_: Metronidazole 750 mg PO tid 5 days

### Orther GI-related conditions

* **Infected necrotic pancreatitis**: Carbapenem IV
  * ACG recommended against prophylactic ABO in severe acute pancreatitis without evidence of infection
* **Acute cholecystitis and acute cholangitis / secondary bacterial peritonitis** (e.g. ruptured appendicitis, bowel ischemia)
  * Ceftiaxone 2 g IV OD + Metronidazole 500 mg IV q 8 hr
* Spontaneous bacterial peritonitis
  * Ceftriaxone 2 g IV OD 5-10 days, OR
  * Cefotaxime 2 g . IV q 8 hr for 5-10 days
* Peritoneal-dialysis (PD)-associated peritonitis: Cefazoline + Ceftazidime via PD

### Urinary Tract Infection

#### Asymptomatic bacteriuria

{% hint style="info" %}
* No signs and symptoms of UTI
* Significant bacteriuria
  * Midstream urine ≥ 10<sup>5</sup> CFU/mL x 1 in men, or 2 consecutive times in women
  * Catheter urine ≥ 10<sup>5</sup> CFU/mL x 1 regardless of sex
{% endhint %}

* Pregnant woman
  * Surveillance UC at 1st ANC --> treat as cystitis based on susceptibility
  * Repeat UC 1-2 wk after treatment
* Plan to undergo **urological procedure with mucosal tearing**
  * Baseline urine culture is recommended
  * Single dose of 30-60 minutes before intervention

#### Acute uncomplicated pyelonephritis in women

* UA and UC **not nessessery**
* Nitrofuranoin dual-released capsule 100 mg IV PO BID x 7 days
* Nitrofurantoin microcrystals 100 mg QID x 7 days

{% hint style="info" %}
ห้ามใช้ nitrofurantoin ในคนท้องไตรมาสที่ 3, upper tract infection, Creatinine clearance < 30, G6PD deficiency
{% endhint %}

* Fosfomycin 3 g PO single dose

{% hint style="info" %}
ห้ามใช้ Fosfomycin ใน upper tract UTI, Creatinine clearance < 10
{% endhint %}

* Alternatives
  * Oral 3rd generation cephalosporin: Cefdinir, Cefditoren, Cefixime, Cefibuten — dosing as previously stated[#oral-antibiotics](frequently-used-antimicrobials.md#oral-antibiotics "mention")
  * Oral Augmentin
  * Single dose aminoglycoside: Gentamicin, Amikacin
  * Ciprofloxacin OR Bactrim x 2 days **ONLY IF SUSCEPTIBLE\*\***

#### Acute uncomplicated pyelonephritis

* **UA, UC all cases** +/- H/C if severe case or sepsis
* Imaging if not response in 72 hr., or recurrent ≥ 4 weeks
  * Rule-out complications such as perinephric abscess
* **Empirical antibiotics**
  * **No sepsis/septic shock without MDR risk:** Ceftriaxone
  * **Sepsis and Septic Shock** **or MDR risk**: Meropenem or Imipenem&#x20;
* **Definitive therapy**
  * Adjust based on urine culture
  * OPAT ot IV-to-PO if possible (even with bacteremia)
* **Duration:** 7 days if fast recovery, may extend to 10-14 days

#### Complicated UTIs

* **UA, UC in all cases**
* Treatment recommendation:
  * Empirical ATBs based on clinical settings and MDR risk
  * Renal abscess or infected renal cyst — prefer Ciprofloxacin, Levofloxacin, or TMP/SMX (if susceptible) due to superior tissue penetration.
* Total duration: at least 10-14 days or more
* Consult surgey in case of undrained abscess or infected renal cyst.

#### Empirical ATBs

* Ceftriaxone 2 g IV q 12 hr
  * Add Vancomycin 15-20 mg/kg IV q 12 hr (post-op neuroSx)
  * Add Ampicillin if
    * age > 50 years
    * Immunodeficiency
    * Alcoholism

#### CNS dosage

* Ceftriaxone 2 g IV OD
* Cefotaxime 2 g IV q 4 hr
* Ampicillin 2 g IV q 4 hr
* Gentamicin 5 mg/kg/day

#### Herpes Simplex Encephalitis

* Acyclovir 10 mg/kg/day q 8 hr x 7-14 days

### Skin and Soft Tissue Infection

* Impetigo or Folliculitis
  * Tropical mupirocin ointment apply TID, or
  * Dicloxacillin 250-500 mg PO qid, or
  * Cephalexine 250-500 mg PO qid
* Cellulitis
  * Dicloxacillin 500 mg PO qid
  * Severe:
    * Cloxacillin 1 - 1.5 g IV q 4 hr, or
    * Cefazolin 1-2 g IV q 8 hr, or
    * Augmentin 1.2 g IV q 8 hr, or
    * Vancomycin, Linezolid, Daptomycin
* Skin abscess
  * Dicloxacillin 500 mg PO qid WITH Clindamycin 300 mg PO tid
    * Oral/Perianal abscess --> consider Augmentin 1 g PO bid
* Necrotizing fasciitis
  * Ceftriaxone + Clindamycin 600 - 900 mg IV q 8 hr
* Pyomyositis
  * Cloxacillin IV or Cefazolin, Ceftriaxone, or Clindamycin

### Septic arthritis

* Non-GC:
  * Duration
    * IV antibiotics: until clinical resolve 1-2 weeks
    * Total duration:
      * Streptococcus spp.: 2-4 weeks
      * Gram-negative bacilli: 4-6 weeks.
      * Salmonella: 12 weeks.
* GPC:
  * Cefazolin 2 g IV q 8 hr, or
  * Cloxacillin 2 g IV q 4 hr
* GNB:
  * Ceftriaxone 2 g IV OD, or
  * Cefotaxime 2 g IV q 8 hr
* Unknown source and cannot identity organism:
  * Ceftriaxone 2 g IV OD
* Suspected anaerobic (foul smell pus, post TKA, trauma):
  * add Clindamycin 600 mg IV q 8 hr (combination with Ceftriaxone)
* Gonococcal disease
  * Ceftriaxone 2 g IV OD, or
  * Ciprofloxacin 400 mg IV q 12 hr for 7-10 days
  * Add Azithromycin 1 g single dose, or Doxycycline 100 mg BID for 7 days (if suspected Chlamydia co-infection)

### Pulmonary tuberculosis

* Pulmonary TB: 2IRZE + 4IR
  * Isoniazid 300 mg/day + Vitamin B6 100 mg/day
  * Rifampicin 10MKDay
  * Pyrazinamide 25MKDay
  * Ethambutol 15 MKDay
  * **Note: WHO 2025 — remove Ethambutol, use Moxifloxacin instead**
* TB meningitis: 2IRZE + 10IR plus dexamethasone 60 mg/day

{% hint style="info" %}
อาจมีการเปลี่ยนแปลง Regimen pulmonary TB เนื่องจาก WHO 2026 TB guideline ได้มีการเปลี่ยน regimen for drug-susceptible TB เป็น IRZM
{% endhint %}

### Tropical infection

#### Malaria

* Primary Regimen
  * Severe: Artesunate IV or Quinine IV
  * Uncomplicated:
    * PF: DHA-PIP 3 days + Primaquine 1 day
    * PV/PO: Chloroquine 3 days + Primaquine 14 days
    * PM/PK: Chloroquine 3 days
* Second line
  * PF
    * Artesunate-pyronaridine 3 days + primaquine 1 day
    * Artemeter-lumefantrine 3 days + primaquine 1 day
    * Quinine sulfate + clindamycin/doxycycline/tetracycline 7 day + primaquine 1 day
  * PV/PO:
    * DHA-PIP 3 days + primaquine 14 days
  * PM/PK:
    * DHA-PIP 3 days

#### Scrub Typhus/Rickettsias

* Doxycycline 100 mg BID x 7 days (C/I in pregnancy)
* 2nd line: Azithromycin 500 mg OD 3 days
* Severe form: Azithromycin IV or Chloramphenicol

#### Leptospirosis

* Mild: as scrub typhus
* Severe form: Penicillin G sodium 1.5 million U q 6 hr, OR
* Ceftriaxone 2 g IV OD

#### Melioidosis

* Ceftazidime 2 g IV q 8 hr for 10-14 days, if not response switch to Meropenem
* Maintanence: TMP/SMX (8-10 MKDay of TMP/SMX) bid for 20 weeks

### Parasitic Infection

* Albendazole (200)
  * 2x1 for 21 days (Gnathostomiasis)
  * 2x2 po x 5 days (Strongyloides, Creeping eruption)
  * 2x2 for 8-30 days (Neurocysticercosis)
  * 2 tab once (Trichuris/Ascaris/Hookwork)
  * 2 tab once then อีก 2 tabs next 2 wk for enterobius
  * 1x2 for 10 days (Capillaria)
* Ivermectin (6) (200 mcg/kg) x 2 days
  * Prolonged to 5-7 days in disseminated strongyloides.
* Praziquantel (600)
  * 10 mg/kg once (Tapeworm)
  * 2x3 for 2 days (Opisthorchis)
* Metronidazole (750) 1x3 for 5 days (Giardia, E. histolytica)

### Viral Infection

* Acyclovir (200, 400, and 800 mg)
  * 4,000 mg/day in Herpes Zoster, severe VZV in adult
    * Acyclovir (800) 1x5 for 7 days
  * 1,000 mg/day in Herpes simplex
    * Acyclovir (200) 1x5 for 5 days
* Warts, Molluscum contangiosum:
  * Imiquimode apply 3 times/week
* Influenzae:
  * Ozeltamivir (75) 1x2 for 5 days
* CMV:
  * Gancyclovir 5 MKDose IV q 12 hr x 2-3 weeks.
